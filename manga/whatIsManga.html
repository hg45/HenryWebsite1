What is manga?

Well, I'm glad you asked. Manga are essentially Japanese graphic novels. They can be short, or they can be in series with hundreds of volumes. Manga is easy to get into, as most of the time there aren't inter-connected multiverses between series leaving you with no idea where to start. You can just pick up the first volume of any series, and start reading. Unlike western comics, manga typically reads from right to left (but it's not very hard to adjust to).

Or, if you prefer the wikipedia definition: Manga (Japanese: 漫画 [maŋga]) are comics or graphic novels originating from Japan. Most manga conform to a style developed in Japan in the late 19th century, and the form has a long prehistory in earlier Japanese art. The term manga is used in Japan to refer to both comics and cartooning. Outside of Japan, the word is typically used to refer to comics originally published in the country.

Manga is published in chapters in magazines and in volumes. There aren't any official English manga magazine publications, however there are many in Japan, such as Shonen Jump. Shonen Jump has an app for official English translations of chapters of various manga series as they release in Japan, and the publisher Kodansha has digital manga chapters available on the Crunchyroll Manga app. English Manga volumes are the way most manga collectors buy manga. Volumes, or tankōbon, are usually around 200 pages and $10, although some editions are significantly larger and more expensive.
